# Kindling Design Document

**Project:** Kindling  
**Version:** 0.1.0 (Planning)  
**Minecraft:** 1.21.x (NeoForge)  
**Status:** Design Phase

---

# Vision

Kindling is a carefully curated Vanilla+ Minecraft experience focused on wonder, exploration, meaningful survival, and cozy creativity.

It is designed to encourage players to slow down, explore the world, build beautiful places, and enjoy the journey rather than rushing toward automation or endgame progression.

The world itself should be the primary source of excitement.

---

# Core Philosophy

Every mod must answer one question:

> **Does this make the world feel more alive?**

If the answer is no, it does not belong in Kindling.

---

# Design Pillars

## 🌲 Wonder

Players should constantly discover places that make them stop and explore.

The world should inspire curiosity.

Examples include:

- breathtaking terrain
- dense forests
- forgotten ruins
- hidden caves
- abandoned villages
- waterfalls
- beautiful rivers

---

## 🏡 Hearth

Building should feel rewarding because players want to create beautiful homes.

Kindling encourages:

- cabins
- inns
- villages
- farms
- gardens
- workshops
- observatories
- lighthouses

rather than massive industrial complexes.

---

## 🌙 Atmosphere

Immersion matters.

The world should feel alive through:

- weather
- ambience
- lighting
- wildlife
- sounds
- foliage
- environmental storytelling

---

## ⚔ Meaningful Survival

Survival should matter without becoming tedious.

Food, travel, weather, and danger should encourage preparation.

Players should never feel punished for exploring.

---

## ✨ Mystery

Exploration should reward curiosity.

Players should discover:

- ancient structures
- hidden paths
- magical forests
- forgotten civilizations
- unusual creatures
- rare landmarks

without relying on quest books.

---

# What Kindling Is NOT

Kindling intentionally avoids:

- giant technology mods
- excessive automation
- kitchen sink design
- progression trees
- unnecessary complexity
- grind for the sake of grind

---

# Building Principles

The world should inspire small, lived-in builds.

Examples include:

- cottages
- riverside homes
- windmills
- docks
- bridges
- towers
- libraries
- greenhouses

---

# Multiplayer Philosophy

Kindling should encourage friends to naturally spread across the landscape and create a connected world.

Villages, roads, waterways, and landmarks should emerge organically.

---

# Technical Goals

- Stable multiplayer
- Excellent performance
- Minimal configuration
- Easy synchronization through Club 115
- Easy updates
- Long-term world compatibility

---

# Mod Selection Rules

Every mod should satisfy at least one of the following:

- Improves exploration
- Improves immersion
- Improves building
- Improves atmosphere
- Improves survival
- Improves performance

If it satisfies none of these, it is excluded.

---

- Building

- Visuals
- Audio

---

# Success Criteria

A successful Kindling world should make players say:

> "I wonder what's over that hill."

rather than

> "How do I rush to endgame?"

The goal is not to add more content.

The goal is to make every moment in the world feel meaningful.
